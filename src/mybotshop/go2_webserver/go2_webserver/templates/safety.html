Autonomous Robot Safety

When deploying autonomous mobile robots, prioritizing safety procedures is essential to prevent accidents and ensure secure operations.

Work Area Safety
  • Maintain a clean and well-lit work area. Cluttered or poorly illuminated spaces can impede the proper functioning of sensors and navigation systems.
  • Avoid operating autonomous mobile robots in explosive atmospheres, such as areas with flammable liquids, gases, or dust.
  • Keep bystanders and unauthorized personnel at a safe distance during robot operation to prevent interference with autonomous navigation.
Electrical Safety
  • Ensure the robot's power system adheres to electrical safety standards. Regularly inspect and maintain power components.
  • Implement mechanisms to protect the robot from adverse weather conditions, such as rain or wet environments.
  • Regularly inspect power cables and connections and replace damaged components promptly.
Navigation Safety
  • Implement obstacle detection and avoidance systems to prevent collisions with objects, people, or other robots.
  • Define and enforce safety zones within the robot's operational area to minimize unintended interactions.
  • Regularly calibrate and test the robot's navigation sensors and systems to ensure accurate performance.
Emergency Response
  • Install an emergency stop mechanism to quickly halt the robot's operation in case of unforeseen circumstances.
  • Clearly mark and communicate emergency stop locations throughout the robot's operational area.
  • Conduct regular emergency response drills to ensure personnel are familiar with procedures.
Data Security & Privacy
  • Implement robust cybersecurity measures to protect the robot's control systems and data from unauthorized access.
  • Ensure compliance with privacy regulations when collecting, storing, or transmitting sensor data.
Human Interaction Safety
  • Integrate sensors and communication systems to detect and respond to the presence of humans in the robot's vicinity.
  • Clearly communicate the robot's operational status and intentions using visual and audible signals.
  • Establish protocols for safe human-robot collaboration in shared workspaces.
Residual Risks
  • Impairment of sensor functionality due to environmental conditions.
  • Risk of collisions in crowded or dynamic environments.
  • Cybersecurity vulnerabilities may exist despite protective measures.
  • Unintended human interactions due to unforeseen circumstances.

Robotic Manipulator Safety

When deploying robotic manipulators, it is imperative to prioritize safety procedures to mitigate risks and ensure secure operations.

Work Area Safety
  • Maintain a clean and well-organized work area. Cluttered or inadequately lit spaces can impede sensor functioning.
  • Avoid operating in hazardous environments with corrosive substances, extreme temperatures, or sharp objects.
  • Ensure only authorized personnel are present during manipulator operation.
Electrical Safety
  • Ensure the manipulator's power system adheres to IEC and ISO electrical safety standards.
  • Implement safeguards to protect from adverse conditions like moisture or extreme temperatures.
  • Regularly inspect power cables and connections, replacing damaged components promptly.
Manipulation Safety
  • Implement collision detection systems to prevent unintended contact with objects, humans, or equipment.
  • Define and enforce safety zones around the manipulator's workspace.
  • Regularly calibrate and test the manipulator's sensors and systems.
Emergency Response
  • Install an emergency stop mechanism to swiftly halt manipulator operation.
  • Clearly mark and communicate emergency stop locations.
  • Conduct regular emergency response drills.
Data Security & Privacy
  • Implement robust cybersecurity measures to safeguard control systems and data.
  • Ensure compliance with privacy regulations for sensor-captured data.
Human Interaction Safety
  • Integrate sensors to detect and respond to human presence.
  • Communicate operational status using visual and audible signals.
  • Establish protocols for safe human-robot collaboration.
Residual Risks
  • Sensor functionality impairment may compromise safety.
  • Risk of collisions during complex manipulation tasks.
  • Unintended human interactions due to unforeseen circumstances.

Battery Safety

Proper battery handling and maintenance is critical for safe robot operation. Lithium-ion batteries require special care.

Charging Safety
  • Only use the manufacturer-provided charger. Third-party chargers may cause fire or damage.
  • Never leave batteries charging unattended. Monitor for unusual heat, swelling, or odors.
  • Charge batteries at room temperature (15-25°C). Avoid extreme conditions.
  • Keep a Class D fire extinguisher nearby. Water can worsen lithium fires.
Handling & Storage
  • Never puncture, crush, drop, or expose batteries to physical damage.
  • Keep batteries dry. Water exposure can cause short circuits.
  • Store batteries away from direct sunlight and heat sources (15-25°C ideal).
  • For long-term storage, maintain batteries at 40-60% charge.
Warning Signs
  • Swelling: Immediately discontinue use if battery appears swollen or deformed.
  • Overheating: If battery becomes excessively hot, disconnect immediately.
  • Unusual Odor: Chemical or burning smells indicate potential failure.
  • Rapid Discharge: Batteries draining unusually fast may have internal shorts.
Disposal
  • Never dispose of batteries in regular trash. They can cause fires in waste facilities.
  • Use designated battery recycling facilities.
  • Tape battery terminals before disposal to prevent short circuits.

Maintenance Safety

Safe maintenance practices protect personnel and preserve robot functionality. Always follow lockout/tagout procedures.

Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)
  • Power Down: Always shut down the robot completely before maintenance.
  • Lock Out: Use physical locks on power disconnects.
  • Tag Out: Attach warning tags indicating who locked out and why.
  • Verify: Attempt to start equipment to verify lockout is effective.
Personal Protective Equipment
  • Eye Protection: Wear safety glasses when working with debris-producing components.
  • Gloves: Use cut-resistant for sharp components, insulated for electrical work.
  • Clothing: Avoid loose clothing and jewelry that could catch in moving parts.
  • Footwear: Wear safety shoes with slip-resistant soles.
Safe Procedures
  • Always refer to official maintenance documentation.
  • Use the buddy system for complex maintenance tasks.
  • Use proper tools for the job. Improvised tools increase risk.
  • Document all maintenance activities and findings.
Software Updates
  • Backup First: Always create backups before updates.
  • Power Supply: Ensure stable power during updates. Use UPS if available.
  • Test After: Thoroughly test robot functionality after updates.

Environmental Conditions

Operating within specified environmental parameters ensures safe and reliable robot performance.

Temperature
  • Operating Range: Typical 0°C to 40°C. Check specifications for your model.
  • High Temperature: Excessive heat causes battery degradation and electronic failures.
  • Low Temperature: Cold reduces battery capacity and affects lubricant viscosity.
Humidity & Water
  • Humidity Range: Typical 20-80% non-condensing.
  • Water Ingress: Check IP rating. IP65+ required for outdoor/wet environments.
  • Condensation: Allow robot to acclimate when moving between temperature zones.
Dust & Particles
  • Dust Protection: Check IP rating. IP6X provides complete dust protection.
  • Regular Cleaning: Clean sensors, vents, and filters regularly in dusty environments.
  • Sensor Occlusion: Dust on sensors (LiDAR, cameras) impairs navigation.
Terrain & Surface
  • Slopes: Check maximum incline rating. Typical AMRs handle 5-10° slopes.
  • Surface Type: Smooth, hard floors are optimal. Carpet/gravel may reduce performance.
  • Slippery Surfaces: Wet or oily floors reduce traction and cause navigation errors.
